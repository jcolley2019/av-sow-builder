3-Way Divisible Room (Rooms 202, 203, and 206)
Room Executive Summary:
This Scope of Work defines the AV technology refresh for the Multi-Purpose Rooms (MPR) at the Centene facility. The project consists of two primary divisible areas: a 3-Way Divisible Room (Rooms 202, 203, and 206) and a 2-Way Divisible Room (Rooms 208 and 209). Within the 3-Way Divisible Room, Room 206 is designated as the primary room and serves as the central video conferencing host when rooms are combined. EOS IT Management Solutions will decommission the existing Crestron control, Biamp DSP and ceiling microphones, AVer cameras, Shure wireless mic infrastructure, Crestron DM-MD8X8 video distribution matrix, Crestron AMP-1200 amplifiers, and the existing Netgear AVB network switch. All decommissioned hardware will be inventoried and handed over to the owner. The new system replaces this infrastructure with a Cisco-native conferencing experience driven by Cisco Room Navigators, a QSC Q-SYS audio backbone, Shure MXA920W-S ceiling microphones with A900-CM ceiling mount kits, new Shure MX412S/C lectern gooseneck microphones, Shure MXW neXt 2 wireless microphones, a QSC Q-SYS NV Series AV-over-IP video distribution system, QSC SPA-Qf 60x4 network amplifiers, a stacked pair of Netgear M4350 AV Line network switches in the 3-way equipment rack, while reusing the existing Crestron Saros IC8T ceiling speakers and projection screens.
Room Environment Overview:
This space consists of three adjacent rooms (202, 203, and 206) separated by movable partitions, with Room 203 in the center and Rooms 202 and 206 on either side. Room 206 serves as the primary room when combined-mode operation is engaged.
Existing Infrastructure to Remain: The system will reuse the existing Crestron Saros IC8T ceiling speakers (12 per room) and the existing OFE projection screens (one per room). All lectern gooseneck microphones, wall plates, partition logic, and rack power distribution are being fully replaced under this scope.
Divisible Logic: Two (2) Extron ECM S10 eBUS Partition Sensors are provided and installed by EOS, one at each of the two partition tracks within the 3-way suite (between Rooms 202–203 and between Rooms 203–206). The partition sensors automate combined-mode state transitions, allowing Rooms 202, 203, and 206 to operate independently or as a unified “All-Hands” space where audio and video are synchronized across all zones with Room 206 serving as the primary host.
Room Video System:
EOS will provide and install (3) Epson PowerLite L690E 3LCD Laser Projectors, 6,500 lumens with 4K Enhancement) (one per room) utilizing Chief SYSAUWP2 Projector Kits with integrated power, displaying onto the existing OFE 159″ diagonal projection screens. Video distribution is provided by an EOS-supplied and installed QSC Q-SYS NV Series AV-over-IP system, replacing the existing Crestron DM-MD8X8 video matrix and HDBaseT receiver infrastructure.
Each room is provisioned with a Q-SYS NV Series encoder/decoder pair on its Cisco Codec Pro G2: one (1) QSC NV-21-HU configured as an encoder on the codec HDMI output (streaming the codec’s program output onto the AV-over-IP network), and one (1) QSC NV-21-HU configured as a decoder on a codec HDMI input (receiving the routed AV-over-IP stream selected for presentation on that codec). Each projector is also fed by one (1) QSC NV-21-HU configured as a decoder located at the projector, taking the routed network stream and outputting HDMI to the Epson projector.

The primary room (Room 206) is provisioned with two (2) additional QSC NV-21-HU decoders, each feeding a separate HDMI input on the Room 206 master Cisco Codec Pro G2. These additional decoders accept the codec output streams from Rooms 202 and 203 during combined-mode operation, allowing the master codec to display content from either secondary room as an auxiliary source.
Each room is also provisioned with one (1) QSC NV-1-H-WE Wall Plate Encoder (PoE-powered) replacing the existing WB1 wall plate, which streams the wall plate HDMI input directly to the network as a Q-SYS native source. All NV video routing is configured in Q-SYS Designer and orchestrated by the Q-SYS Cores as part of the divisible-room state logic. Two (2) QSC NV-32-H (Core Capable) 4K60 4:4:4 Network Video Endpoints are also installed in the 3-way equipment rack, held as spare AV-over-IP endpoints and available for ad-hoc encoding of existing or future legacy HDMI sources as required.
Lectern HDMI and USB-C inputs are provided by an EOS-supplied and installed Sound Control Technologies RTK-AM1-C BYOM Table Extension Kit at each lectern (one per room). The RTK-AM1-C provides power, HDMI, Ethernet, USB, and microphone signal transport between the table and the Cisco Codec Pro G2 over a single communication cable run, supporting 4K/60 HDMI and USB-C BYOM connectivity. The USB-C path at the lectern is provided for laptop content sharing only, it is not intended to support general-purpose USB peripheral use beyond presentation source sharing. The RTK-AM RX module in the rack uses ETHERNET 1 to the network switch (ETHERNET 2 is unused). Each kit includes its companion TX/RX modules, WPS-30V2A 60W power supply, WPS-48V5.73A power supply, USB-A to RS-232 cable, locking USB-C cables (1m and 2m), and required IP control and HDMI patch cables.
Room Audio System:
Audio for conferencing and local reinforcement is managed via EOS-provided and installed (2) Shure MXA920W-S Ceiling Array Microphones per room (six total across the 3-way suite) with Shure A900-CM ceiling mount kits. The MXA920W-S arrays provide full coverage of the 39′ x 34′ room footprint with onboard IntelliMix DSP delivering acoustic echo cancellation, automixing, and beamforming optimized by Shure. Each MXA920W-S publishes lobe activity metadata to the Q-SYS Core for use in camera tracking decisions sent to the active conferencing codec via xAPI.
EOS will provide and install new Shure MX412S/C 12-inch gooseneck cardioid condenser microphones for the lectern positions, replacing all existing OFE goosenecks. Each MX412S/C ships with an attached XLR preamp, shock and flange mount, snap-fit foam windscreen, mute switch, and LED status indicator, and is paired with a Shure A400SMXLR A400SM shock mount and A400XLR XLR insert adapter kit. The new MX412S/C goosenecks terminate to the Q-SYS Core via a QSC QIO-ML2x2 Q-SYS network peripheral (one per room) providing two (2) mic/line inputs and two (2) line outputs each.
Wireless microphones are provided via EOS-supplied (1) Shure MXWAPXD2 MXW neXt 2 Access Point Transceiver per room (3 total, Z10 frequency band), wall-mounted behind the lectern. Each MXWAPXD2 includes integrated battery charging and IntelliMix DSP, supports 2 simultaneous DECT 1.9 GHz wireless channels, and connects to the Q-SYS Core via Dante. Each room is equipped with (1) Shure MXW2/SM58 handheld transmitter and (1) Shure MXW1X bodypack transmitter with a Shure WL185 cardioid lavalier microphone.

EOS will provide and install (1) QSC Q-SYS Core 24f digital signal processor per room (3 total for the 3-way suite) located in the 3-way equipment rack, with (1) QSC SL-DAN-64-P Dante 64x64 license per Core. The Q-SYS Core handles all audio AEC, automixing, voice lift, room combine/divide logic, and integration with the Cisco Codec Pro G2 over analog audio handoff. The Q-SYS Cores are linked via Q-SYS System Link for combined-mode operation, presenting the active codec with a unified mix bus from all active microphones in the combined space. The Q-SYS Cores handle all in-room AEC, automixing, gain structure, and microphone management, presenting the active Cisco Codec Pro G2 with conferencing audio.
EOS will provide and install (3) QSC SPA-Qf 60x4 Q-SYS Network Amplifiers in the 3-way equipment rack, one per room, configured with all four channels bridged in pairs to deliver 120W per zone at 70V. Each amplifier provides two bridged 70V zones, a front-of-room zone and a rear-of-room zone per room, driving the existing Crestron Saros IC8T ceiling speakers for voice lift reinforcement. Voice lift is provided to the ceiling speakers in both standalone and combined operating modes. In standalone mode, the rear-of-room zone reinforces talkers from the front for rear seating intelligibility within that room. In combined mode, all ceiling speakers across the combined space remain active and voice lift continues to operate across the unified mix bus so that talkers anywhere in the combined space are reinforced through all ceiling speakers, maintaining intelligibility for rear seating positions throughout the combined room.
Room Conferencing System:
EOS will provide and install (3) Cisco Codec Pro G2 systems within the 3-way equipment rack (one per room) as part of the Cisco Room Kit Pro G2 with Room Vision PTZ (First Light) bundle. Each room includes one (1) Cisco Room Navigator wall-mount touch panel (CS-T10-WM-L-K9, First Light) installed outside each room for scheduling, which connects directly to the host Cisco Codec Pro G2 network port (not the AV switch), covered under a Cisco CON-SNBD-CST10LKW Success Tracks Standard with 8x5 NBD support contract (one per room), and one (1) Cisco Room Navigator table-mount unit at the lectern for in-room meeting control, which connects to the network switch.
Each room is equipped with (2) Cisco Room Vision PTZ Cameras (Arctic White), one rear-wall presenter camera and one front-wall room-facing camera. Each camera connects to its host Cisco Codec Pro G2 via a Sound Control Technologies RC5+ UNI Camera Extender kit, (2) RC5+ UNI kits per room, one per camera, delivering 66W PoE++, uncompressed 4K60 4:4:4 video, and IP/Ethernet camera control over a single CAT cable run up to 100 meters between the camera and the codec’s PoE++ camera port (not the AV switch). Each RC5+ UNI kit ships with paired CE (Camera End) and HE (Head End) modules, HDMI and UTP camera cables, RJ45 IP control cable, and a WPS-48 120W power supply. In standalone-room operation, Q-SYS uses MXA920W-S lobe metadata combined with Cisco xAPI commands to switch the active camera based on talker location, providing cinematic camera switching between the presenter and audience views of that room without requiring third-party camera tracking hardware. Combined-mode operation is defined in the Combined-Mode Operation section below.
Room Control System:
Room and meeting control is provided by the Cisco Room Navigator in each room, eliminating the need for a third-party control processor. The Cisco Codec Pro G2 communicates with the Q-SYS Core 24f via xAPI for source selection, camera switching, room state changes, and video routing commands. The (2) Extron ECM S10 eBUS Partition Sensors installed at the partition tracks between Rooms 202–203 and 203–206 feed real-time partition state into the Q-SYS Cores via (2) QSC QIO-GP8x8 Q-SYS Control I/O Expanders mounted in the ceiling plenum of the 3-way suite, each providing 8 logic inputs and 8 logic outputs.
The Q-SYS Cores orchestrate the audio combine/divide logic and matched video routing through the QSC Q-SYS NV Series video fabric. Screen and projector control commands are routed from the Q-SYS Cores through EOS-provided and installed QSC QIO-LVR4 Q-SYS Control I/O Expanders (one per room) providing 4 dry-contact relay outputs each for projector lift, screen control, partition lock signaling, or other contact-closure-driven endpoints as required by the existing OFE projection screen infrastructure.
Combined-Mode Operation:
Combined-mode operation is engaged automatically when the ECM S10 partition sensors detect that the movable partitions between Rooms 202, 203, and 206 are open. When engaged, the Q-SYS Core in Room 206 assumes the role of master and the Cisco Codec Pro G2 in Room 206 becomes the primary conferencing host. The Cisco Codec Pro G2 has sufficient HDMI inputs to accept auxiliary content feeds from the secondary room codecs, which are routed to the master codec via the QSC NV-21-HU encoder-to-decoder paths on the AV-over-IP VLAN. Ceiling microphone audio from Rooms 202 and 203 is consolidated to the master codec via Dante audio routing through the Q-SYS Cores so audio coverage spans all combined zones. Cisco Room Vision PTZ cameras are paired one-to-one with their host codec by hardware design and are not transferred between codecs during combined-mode operation; the (2) cameras assigned to Room 206 are the only PTZ cameras available to the active conferencing session when rooms are combined. Each Cisco Room Navigator remains paired with its host codec and is not reassigned between codecs during combined mode. Cross-room camera routing, camera reassignment via VLAN switching, audio-threshold-driven cross-room camera switching, side-by-side or composed video layouts of multiple room camera feeds, and Q-SYS cross-room camera tracking logic are explicitly out of scope for this system. In combined mode, only the Cisco Room Navigator in the master room (Room 206) is operable as the meeting control surface; the Cisco Room Navigators in the secondary rooms (Rooms 202 and 203) are locked from accepting end-user input so users in the secondary rooms cannot connect to, modify, or interrupt the active master conferencing session. Similarly, only the master room input sources (the Room 206 lectern HDMI/USB-C inputs via the Sound Control Technologies RTK-AM1-C Table Hub and the Room 206 QSC NV-1-H-WE wall plate) are active for content sharing on the master codec; the corresponding lectern and wall plate inputs in the secondary rooms are disabled in combined mode to prevent inadvertent source contention or interruption of the master conferencing session. All ceiling speakers across the combined space remain active in combined mode; the Q-SYS Cores mix the unified microphone bus to all ceiling speaker zones simultaneously so voice lift is maintained for rear seating positions throughout the combined 3-way room.
Room Conferencing Switch:
EOS will provide and install (2) Netgear M4350-44M4X4V (MSM4352-100NES) Fully Managed AV Line network switches deployed as a stacked pair in the 3-way equipment rack, providing 88 RJ45 ports total via SFP28 DAC stacking. Each switch is powered by (2) Netgear APS2000W 2000W Modular Power Supplies for full PoE++ budget and dual-PSU power redundancy (four APS2000W total in the 3-way rack). The switch stack provides network connectivity and 802.3bt PoE+ / PoE++ power for the MXA920W-S microphones, MXWAPXD2 wireless access points, QSC NV-21-HU video endpoints, QSC NV-1-H-WE wall plate encoders, Cisco Codec Pro G2 systems, Cisco Room Navigator table units, RTK-AM RX modules, QSC SPA-Qf 60x4 network amplifiers, QIO peripherals, and Q-SYS Cores. The Cisco Room Vision PTZ cameras and the Cisco Room Navigator wall-mount scheduling panels connect directly to the host Cisco Codec Pro G2 and do not consume switch ports. The Netgear M4350 AV Line includes pre-configured AV-over-IP profiles for Q-SYS, Dante, and AES67, simplifying VLAN segmentation, IGMP snooping with querier, QoS, and PTP configuration. Network switch will connect to the OFE corporate network via the SFP+ uplink port. Port requirements, VLAN segmentation for audio/Dante/Q-LAN, AV-over-IP, conferencing, and control traffic, and uplink media coordination will be reviewed with the Owner’s IT Department.
VLAN segmentation is planned as follows, subject to Centene IT confirmation: VLAN 10 “CORP-AV” for the Cisco Codec Pro G2, Cisco Room Navigator table panel, and corporate Webex/Teams uplink; VLAN 20 “AV-CONTROL” for the RTK-AM1 RX modules and Q-SYS administration; VLAN 30 “Q-LAN” for the Q-SYS Cores Q-LAN port, QIO modules, and SPA-Qf amplifiers; VLAN 40 “DANTE” for the Shure microphones, MXWAPXD2 access points, and Q-SYS Cores Dante port; VLAN 50 “AV-OVER-IP” for the NV-21-HU encoders and decoders and NV-1-H-WE wall plates; and VLAN 99 “MGMT” for switch and device management. The 3-way stacked pair is engineered with spare port and PoE headroom reserved per-VLAN for downstream additions.
Room Rack, Power, and Peripherals:
All active processing, switching, and network transport hardware for the 3-way divisible room is centralized inside the 3-way equipment rack. At each individual room, EOS will provide and install (1) Middle Atlantic RLNK-215 15A, 2-outlet IP-controlled power conditioner (3 total for the 3-way suite) for local control of the in-room projector and other room-side powered devices. In the 3-way equipment rack, EOS will provide and install IP-controlled rack power distribution (Middle Atlantic RLNK series) sized to support all rack-mounted electronics for the 3-way suite, replacing the existing rack vertical power strips. Owner is responsible for all building power outlets, dedicated circuits, data ports, and network pathways required for the audio, video, and conferencing systems to operate properly. EOS will coordinate requirements with the Owner and IT Department.
